02

How the thinking moved

The order matters — the insurance direction only emerged because we first built the industrial one and then heard what the insurers actually care about.

Starting point

The reusable pattern

Looka is a room of per-member agent panels. Frank's voice orb (OpenAI Realtime over WebRTC) already shipped and proved the loop: browser panel → /api relay → LLM + tools → one spoken answer. That pattern is what let us try on a whole use case in an afternoon instead of a week.

Move 01 · Industrial

The "Iron Man HUD" for operations

First exploration. Reframe — not rebuild — the room as a heads-up display for a factory floor. Ten agents (HazardEye, FloorBoss, SpecOracle, LogScribe, GhostPlan, RemoteHand, BinSeeker, ShiftPulse, TwinCheck, DrawDown), shortlisted to a four-panel demo arc See → Read → Ask → Log. Same PICO build → a second $600 pot.

Move 02 · The insight

What insurers actually want

Feedback from Dinesh and the insurance team flipped the frame: the flashy spatial field demo (flood-scan through glasses) is not what insurers buy. Their pain is the back office — contracts, forms, email, the claims queue. "Make things more efficient" is the feature. And it's the least-contested, highest-opportunity track on the board.

Move 03 · Insurance

Point the room at the operator's desk

Pivoted the exploration. Ten desk agents that do in seconds what took days (ClaimTriage, PolicyParser, FraudSniff, MailDrafter, FormFiller, IntakeScribe, CoverageMatch, RenewalRadar, SettleMate, AuditTrail), shortlisted to Sort → Read → Check → Reply — one claim from overnight inbox to filed answer.

Next · Open

Replace guesses with their real pain

The highest-leverage move isn't more ideas — it's ten minutes with the ERGO people at demo day. Six sharp questions are queued in the insurance doc; their answers re-rank the ten and lock the four. Then we re-cut and rewrite the trailer's opening line to their exact words.

03

The one line to remember

Cross-cutting insight

The winning move for a horizontal agent-room isn't the flashiest field demo — it's finding the industry whose boring, expensive, back-office work our panels can quietly delete. For insurance, that's paperwork. Same room, pointed at the pain.

Source · XRCC ERGO brief + Dinesh & insurance-team feedback, 2026-07-01
